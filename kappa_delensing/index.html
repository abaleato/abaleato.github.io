In what follows, we assume r=1, such that the cross-spectrum between g and I has to be measured on the patch where g, I and kappa all overlap. The sign of gamma identifies regions where variance cancellation can (in theory) lead to improved S/N. We can also visualize gamma itself. However, the actual gain in S/N is limited by the ratio of uncertainty after delensing (including the systematic error incurred when restoring the signal), to that before delensing.
